Counting: a valid path is an arrangement of w R's and h D's with no "RR". Lay down the h D's first. This creates h+1 slots (gaps) around them: one before the first D, one between each adjacent pair, and one after the last D. To avoid any "RR", each slot may hold AT MOST one R. So we must choose w of the h+1 slots to receive a single R, giving C(h+1, w) valid paths. (C(h+1, w) = 0 when w > h+1, which is the impossible case; and C(h+1, 0) = 1 covers w=0.)

Evaluate:
- w=0, h=0: C(1, 0) = 1
- w=0, h=6: C(7, 0) = 1
- w=1, h=0: C(1, 1) = 1
- w=5, h=2: C(3, 5) = 0 (impossible: 5 R's cannot fit in only 3 slots without an "RR")
- w=4, h=6: C(7, 4) = 35
- w=3, h=3: C(4, 3) = 4

(As a sanity check, the ordinary-lattice-path values C(w+h,w) would be 1, 1, 1, 21, 210, 20 — the last three are wrong here because they ignore the no-"RR" rule.)
