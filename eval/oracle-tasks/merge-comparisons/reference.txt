Two families of bounds.

Adversary lower bound for the standard two-pointer pairwise merge is m + n - 1:
- (1,1): 1; (2,2): 3; (3,5): 7; (1,9): 9; (7,7): 13.

Information-theoretic minimum is ceil(log2(C(m+n, m))), computed with exact integer arithmetic:
- (1,1): C(2,1)=2  -> ceil(log2 2)=1
- (2,2): C(4,2)=6  -> ceil(log2 6)=3
- (3,5): C(8,3)=56 -> ceil(log2 56)=6
- (1,9): C(10,1)=10 -> ceil(log2 10)=4
- (7,7): C(14,7)=3432 -> 2^11=2048 < 3432 <= 4096=2^12, so 12.

The lopsided (1,9) and the (7,7) case are exactly where the two bounds part ways (4 vs 9, and 12 vs 13).
